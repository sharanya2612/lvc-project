MongoDB

MongoDB is a popular, open-source, document-oriented NoSQL database management system. Unlike traditional relational databases, MongoDB stores data in flexible, JSON-like documents, making it easier to scale, store complex data types, and manage large datasets.

MongoDB is designed for high performance, high availability, and easy scalability. It is used in many modern applications, ranging from small projects to large-scale enterprise solutions. Some of its features include flexible data models, powerful querying capabilities, and support for large-scale data replication and sharding.

One of the key advantages of MongoDB is its ability to handle unstructured or semi-structured data. This makes it a great choice for applications that require rapid iteration or have evolving data structures. It also provides features like automatic failover and built-in replication, ensuring reliability and uptime.
